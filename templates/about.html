Our Technology

EfficientNetB4

We use Google's state-of-the-art EfficientNetB4 architecture, fine-tuned on dermatological images for maximum accuracy.

Training Data

Our model is trained on the HAM10000 dataset, containing over 10,000 dermatoscopic images of pigmented skin lesions.

Performance

Our model achieves over 90% accuracy in distinguishing between different types of skin lesions, including malignant melanoma.

Skin Cancer Types

Actinic Keratoses (akiec)

A precancerous growth caused by sun damage that may develop into squamous cell carcinoma if left untreated.

Medium Risk

Basal Cell Carcinoma (bcc)

The most common type of skin cancer, usually appearing as a pearly or waxy bump, or a flat, flesh-colored or brown scar-like lesion.

High Risk

Melanoma (mel)

The most dangerous form of skin cancer, which can spread to other parts of the body if not detected early.

Very High Risk

Benign Lesions

Including Melanocytic Nevi (nv), Benign Keratosis (bkl), Dermatofibroma (df), and Vascular Lesions (vasc).

Low Risk
